### Reasoning
The task is to determine the change in angular velocity as the skater pulls in her arms. This involves the conservation of angular momentum. The key steps are:
1. **Angular Momentum Conservation**: When the skater pulls in her arms, her moment of inertia decreases, causing her angular velocity to increase to conserve angular momentum. The relationship is \( I_1 \omega_1 = I_2 \omega_2 \), where \( I \) is the moment of inertia and \( \omega \) is the angular velocity.
2. **Moment of Inertia for a Capsule**: The skater is modeled as a capsule. For rotation about its symmetry axis (z-axis), the moment of inertia is \( I = \frac{1}{2} m r^2 \), where \( m \) is the mass and \( r \) is the radius.
3. **Radii Assumption**: The initial scene provides a capsule radius of 0.1 m. To simulate "arms extended" (larger moment of inertia), we assume an initial radius of 0.15 m. After pulling in, the radius is 0.1 m. This is a reasonable approximation since pulling in arms reduces the effective radius.
4. **Ratio Calculation**: The change in angular velocity is given by \( \Delta \omega = \omega_2 - \omega_1 \). Using conservation:
   \[
   \omega_2 = \omega_1 \frac{I_1}{I_2} = \omega_1 \frac{\frac{1}{2} m r_1^2}{\frac{1}{2} m r_2^2} = \omega_1 \left( \frac{r_1}{r_2} \right)^2
   \]
   Thus:
   \[
   \Delta \omega = \omega_1 \left( \left( \frac{r_1}{r_2} \right)^2 - 1 \right)
   \]
   Substituting \( r_1 = 0.15 \) and \( r_2 = 0.1 \):
   \[
   \Delta \omega = \omega_1 \left( \left( \frac{0.15}{0.1} \right)^2 - 1 \right) = \omega_1 (2.25 - 1) = 1.25 \omega_1
   \]
5. **Need for Initial Angular Velocity**: The change \( \Delta \omega \) depends on \( \omega_1 \), which must be measured from the simulator after setting up the initial spinning state with arms extended (radius 0.15 m).
