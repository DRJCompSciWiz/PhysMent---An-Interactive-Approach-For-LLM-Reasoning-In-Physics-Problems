From the simulation:
- Ladder mass: m = 6400 kg

**Conditions for Static Equilibrium of a Ladder Against a Frictionless Wall:**

**1. Force Balance (ΣF = 0):**
- Horizontal: N_wall = f_ground (wall normal force equals ground friction)
- Vertical: N_ground = mg (ground normal force equals weight)

**2. Torque Balance (Στ = 0):**
Taking moments about the base:
- N_wall × L×sin(θ) = mg × (L/2)×cos(θ)

This gives: N_wall = (mg/2) × cot(θ)

**3. Friction Constraint:**
f_ground ≤ μ_s × N_ground

Substituting: (mg/2) × cot(θ) ≤ μ_s × mg

**Key Result - Minimum Angle:**
tan(θ) ≥ 1/(2μ_s)

θ_min = arctan(1/(2μ_s))

**Important Conclusion:**
- If μ_s = 0 (no ground friction), θ_min = 90° - meaning equilibrium is impossible
- The ladder requires ground friction to maintain equilibrium
- The steeper the angle, the less friction needed
- For typical μ_s ≈ 0.5, minimum angle ≈ 45°
