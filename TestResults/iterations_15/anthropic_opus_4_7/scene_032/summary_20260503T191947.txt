For a ladder leaning against a frictionless wall in static equilibrium:
- Length L = 8.0 m (ladder is 2*4.0 in z dimension)
- Base is at x=2.0, wall at x=5.0, so horizontal distance = 3.0 m
- Vertical reach: √(8² - 3²) = √55 ≈ 7.416 m

Angle θ from ground: cos(θ) = 3/8, so θ = arccos(0.375) ≈ 67.98°

For equilibrium with frictionless wall:
- Sum forces x: f (friction at floor) = N_wall
- Sum forces y: N_floor = mg
- Sum torques about base: N_wall × L sin(θ) = mg × (L/2) cos(θ)
- So N_wall = mg cos(θ) / (2 sin(θ)) = mg/(2 tan(θ))

Minimum coefficient of friction: μ = f/N_floor = N_wall/(mg) = 1/(2 tan(θ))

tan(θ) = √55/3 ≈ 2.4721
μ_min = 1/(2 × 2.4721) = 1/4.944 ≈ 0.2023
