**How damping ratio affects amplitude decay:**

The damping ratio (ζ) determines the nature of amplitude decay in a damped oscillator:

1. **Underdamped (ζ < 1)**: Amplitude decays exponentially as A(t) = A₀ × e^(-ζωₙt), with the system oscillating around equilibrium. Lower ζ results in more oscillations before settling.

2. **Critically damped (ζ = 1)**: The system returns to equilibrium as fast as possible without oscillating. Amplitude decays exponentially without oscillation.

3. **Overdamped (ζ > 1)**: The system returns to equilibrium slowly without oscillating. Amplitude decays as a sum of two exponential terms with time constants τ₁ and τ₂. Higher ζ results in slower return to equilibrium.

The key relationship is: **Amplitude decays exponentially, with the decay rate proportional to the damping ratio for underdamped systems, but larger damping ratios (overdamped) result in slower non-oscillatory decay.**
