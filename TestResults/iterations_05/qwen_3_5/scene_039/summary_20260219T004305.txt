The system shows rapid amplitude decay - the mass goes from z=1.0 to z=0.3 (equilibrium) within about 1 second with minimal oscillation. This indicates a **critically damped or overdamped system** (ζ ≥ 1).

For a damped oscillator, the amplitude decay follows: A(t) = A₀ × e^(-ζωₙt)

Where:
- ζ = damping ratio
- ωₙ = natural frequency

The relationship between damping ratio and amplitude decay:
- **Higher ζ** → Faster exponential decay rate
- **Lower ζ** (underdamped) → Slower decay with oscillations
- **ζ = 1** (critically damped) → Fastest return to equilibrium without oscillation
- **ζ > 1** (overdamped) → Slower return without oscillation

From the observed behavior (rapid decay to equilibrium in ~1 second with no visible oscillations), the damping ratio appears to be approximately **1.0** (critically damped), which provides the fastest amplitude decay without overshooting.
