**Analysis of damping ratio effects on amplitude decay:**

The system exhibited **overdamped or critically damped behavior** (damping ratio ζ ≥ 1), characterized by:
1. **No oscillations** - the mass never crossed the equilibrium position
2. **Monotonic decay** - amplitude decreased continuously without overshoot
3. **Exponential envelope** - even without oscillations, the decay follows an exponential pattern

For a damped oscillator, the damping ratio affects amplitude decay as follows:
- **Underdamped (ζ < 1)**: Amplitude decays exponentially as A(t) = A₀ × e^(-ζωₙt), with oscillations
- **Critically damped (ζ = 1)**: Fastest return to equilibrium without oscillations
- **Overdamped (ζ > 1)**: Slower return to equilibrium without oscillations

The amplitude decay rate is proportional to the damping ratio - higher ζ means faster energy dissipation (up to critical damping), but values above critical actually slow the return to equilibrium.
