Based on my analysis, this is a theoretical question about simple harmonic motion. Let me explain:

In simple harmonic motion, when a mass is displaced by 0.2 m from its equilibrium position and released from rest (zero initial velocity):

1. The displacement of 0.2 m represents the maximum displacement from equilibrium
2. By definition, the amplitude (A) of oscillation is the maximum displacement from the equilibrium position
3. Since the mass starts at 0.2 m with zero velocity, this IS the amplitude

The amplitude is determined by initial conditions, not by the spring constant or mass (those determine the frequency/period of oscillation).

For a mass starting at maximum displacement with zero velocity:
- x(t) = A·cos(ωt)
- At t=0: x(0) = A = 0.2 m
