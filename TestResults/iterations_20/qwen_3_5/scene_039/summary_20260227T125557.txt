I've been tracking the amplitude decay of the damped oscillator over time. The z-coordinate shows the amplitude decreasing from 1.0 to approximately 0.974 over 0.072 seconds. 

For a damped harmonic oscillator, the amplitude decays exponentially as:
A(t) = A₀ * e^(-ζω₀t)

where ζ is the damping ratio and ω₀ is the natural frequency. The damping ratio directly determines the decay rate - higher ζ means faster amplitude decay.

From my observations:
- Initial amplitude: 1.0
- At t=0.072s: amplitude ≈ 0.9739

Using the exponential decay formula:
0.9739 = e^(-ζω₀ * 0.072)
ln(0.9739) = -ζω₀ * 0.072
ζω₀ = -ln(0.9739)/0.072 ≈ 0.0264/0.072 ≈ 0.367

The damping ratio ζ affects the amplitude decay rate proportionally. The decay constant (ζω₀) I calculated is approximately 0.367 s⁻¹.
