To solve this lever equilibrium problem, we need to place an additional weight on the beam such that the system remains balanced. The beam is pivoted at the fulcrum at (0,0,1), with existing weights at (5,0,1.1) and (2,0,1.1). The beam extends from x=0 to x=6 (center at x=3). For equilibrium, the net torque about the fulcrum must be zero.

### Approach
1. **Calculate Masses**: Since the masses aren't provided directly, we use the `get_parameters` tool to retrieve the masses of the beam and existing weights. The additional weight will have the same density and size as the existing weights, so its mass will be identical.
2. **Determine Current Torque**: Compute the torque contribution from each object (beam and existing weights) about the fulcrum at x=0. All current forces produce clockwise torque.
3. **Equilibrium Condition**: The additional weight must create counterclockwise torque to balance the system. Using torque balance, we solve for the position \(x\) where the additional weight should be placed.
4. **Simulate Equilibrium**: After placing the weight, attach the beam to the fulcrum and simulate briefly to verify stability.

### Solution Steps
1. Retrieve masses of the beam (object_1), weight1 (object_2), and weight2 (object_3).
2. The additional weight (mass \(M\)) must be placed at a position \(x\) such that:
   \[
   \text{Torque due to weight1} + \text{Torque due to weight2} + \text{Torque due to beam} + \text{Torque due to additional weight} = 0
   \]
   \[
   (M \cdot g \cdot 5) + (M \cdot g \cdot 2) + (M_{\text{beam}} \cdot g \cdot 3) + (M \cdot g \cdot x) = 0
   \]

   \[
   x = -\frac{(5M + 2M + 3M_{\text{beam}})}{M}
